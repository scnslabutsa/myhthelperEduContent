Health information, in print or online, should come from trusted, credible sources.

</p>	

Government agencies, hospitals, universities, and medical journals and books providing evidence-based information are trustworthy. Too often, other sources can provide misleading or incorrect information. If a source makes claims that are too good to be true, rememberâ€”they usually are.

There are many websites, books, and magazines that provide health information to the public, but not all of them are trustworthy.
